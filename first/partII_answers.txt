1. What does the man command do? Type in man rm. How do you scroll and get out?
    - The man command is like a manual for whatever other command you want more information on.
    - After typing in man rm you get a page breaking down its name, synopsis, decription, etc.
    - You can scroll using your arrow keys or type in h to see other information including more ways to scroll.
    - You get out by typing q, among other ways.

2. Look at the man page for ls. What does the -l flag do? What does the -a flag do?
    - The -l flag uses a long listing format.
    - The -a or --all flag does not ignore entires starting with ".".

3. How do you jump between words in the terminal?
    - You can hold "alt" and move with the left and right arrows.

4. How do you get to the end of a line in terminal?
    - You can hold "ctrl" and the hit the "e" key.

5. How do you move your cursor to the beginning in terminal?
    - You can hold "ctrl" and hit the "a" key.

6. How do you delete a word (without pressing backspace multiple times) in terminal?
    - You can hold "alt" and press the "backspace" key.

7. What is the difference between a terminal and shell?
    - A terminal is the wrapping program/application that runs a shell.
    - A shell is the actual program responsible for processing commands and returning outputs.

8. What is an absolute path?
    - Like a permanent address, a path from the route directory leading directly to the file.

9. What is an relative path?
    - A path from the current directory not the root directory.

10. What is a flag? Give three examples of flags you have used.
    - A flag is a signal to add additional functions to the command you're running.
    - We've seen ls -l for long listing
    - We've seen ls -a for listing all files
    - We've seen cp -r for recursively copying a folder

11. What do the r and f flags do with the rm command?
    - Both the -r and -f flags will force a recursive removal of the directory in question.
